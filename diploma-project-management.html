Programme Summary

In a world of constant change, skilled project managers are essential to every industry. This diploma equips you with the fundamental principles and practices of project management, from initiation and planning to execution and closure. It is ideal for aspiring project coordinators and managers in sectors like IT, construction, NGOs, and business.

A Diploma in Project Management can advance your career, increase your earnings, or help you find a job you enjoy. At KNS College, your existing knowledge can accelerate your progress through the program. With our online and hybrid courses, you have complete flexibility there's no need to log in at specific times. You control your learning schedule. Plus, you'll graduate with both a diploma and leading industry certifications, all included in your tuition. Prepare for a rewarding and high-paying career by earning your diploma in project management from KNS College.

Top Information Technology Certifications at No Extra Cost

Credentials matter in the IT field and showcasing both your skills and achievements is essential. At KNS College, we make it easy for you to earn a Diploma alongside valuable industry certifications. Every one of our IT Programmes includes preparation for top certification exams at no additional cost, since exam fees are covered by your tuition. This means you'll graduate with more than just a diploma; you'll also have industry-recognized certifications to enhance your resume, increase your earning potential, and help you stand out to employers even before you finish your program.

This programme prepares you for the PMI Project Management Ready certification, validating your skills for entry-level project management roles. Learning Outcomes

Upon successful completion of this diploma, you will be able to:

  • Initiate, plan, execute, monitor, and close projects effectively.
  • Manage project scope, schedule, budget, and resources.
  • Identify and mitigate project risks and ensure quality standards are met.
  • Lead project teams and manage stakeholder communications.
  • Apply both traditional (Waterfall) and modern (Agile/Scrum) project management methodologies.
  • Utilize project management software and tools for planning and tracking.

Programme Structure

This two-year programme follows a semester structure (2 per year, 15 weeks each). The expected study time is 3-5 hours per course per week. Assessment is based on Exams (40%), a comprehensive Project Portfolio (40%), and Coursework (20%).
